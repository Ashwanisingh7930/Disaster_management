Earthquake

An earthquake is a sudden shaking or trembling of the earth's surface. It is caused by the movement of tectonic plates beneath the Earth's surface. Earthquakes can be very destructive and can cause buildings to collapse, roads to be damaged and people to be injured or killed. It is important to be prepared for an earthquake by having an emergency kit ready and knowing what to do during an earthquake.

Hurricane

A hurricane is a large, rotating storm that forms over warm ocean waters. Hurricanes can cause strong winds, heavy rain, flooding, and storm surges that can damage homes, businesses, and other infrastructure. It is important to prepare for a hurricane by having an emergency kit ready, knowing evacuation routes, and securing your home before the storm hits.

Volcano

A volcano is an opening in the Earth's crust that allows molten rock, ash, and gas to escape from deep below the surface. Volcanoes can be very dangerous and can cause widespread destruction. It is important to be prepared for a volcanic eruption by having an emergency kit ready, knowing evacuation routes, and staying informed about the latest updates from authorities.
